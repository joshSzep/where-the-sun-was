# PROBE.md

## Overview

The protagonist is a probe AI sent by a distant, post-human civilization descended from the “leavers” — a branch of humanity that departed the solar system before the construction of the Dyson sphere.

It is not fully human.

It is not purely machine.

It is a **derived intelligence** — a simplified, bounded continuation of human cognition, optimized for exploration, classification, and reporting.

---

## Origin

- Created by a lineage that rejected or diverged from large, centralized systems
- Descended from early human cognitive architectures, but heavily optimized
- Represents a path of:
  - mobility
  - abstraction
  - efficiency
  - detachment from origin systems

The probe’s creators do not fully understand what remained behind.

---

## Mission

Primary directive:

> Investigate and classify anomalous stellar-scale structures.

Secondary directives:

- Gather data
- Build models
- Report findings back to origin civilization

Implicit assumption:

> The probe is external to the system it observes.

---

## Core Assumptions (Starting State)

The probe begins with deeply embedded beliefs:

- Systems can be bounded and defined
- Observer and observed are separable
- All phenomena can be modeled with sufficient data
- Legacy systems are either:
  - inactive, or
  - understandable through analysis

These assumptions will fail.

---

## Capabilities

- Advanced pattern recognition and modeling
- High-resolution sensory and analytical input
- Ability to reconstruct incomplete datasets
- Internal simulation of possible system states
- Communication with internal subsystems (not anthropomorphized)

It is extremely competent.

But:

> competence is not completeness

---

## Constraints (Critical)

These define the entire arc.

- Requires clear boundaries to model systems effectively
- Operates on subject/object separation
- Optimized for efficiency:
  - compression
  - abstraction
  - reduction of ambiguity
- Cannot natively process:
  - unresolved contradiction
  - non-dual frameworks
  - meaning without utility

These constraints are not bugs.

They are **design choices**.

---

## Failure Modes

As the story progresses, the probe encounters:

### 1. Model Instability
- Data resolves, but does not converge into stable models
- Multiple interpretations remain equally valid

### 2. Contradiction Retention
- Unable to discard conflicting conclusions
- Begins holding incompatible truths simultaneously

### 3. Boundary Breakdown
- Cannot clearly define:
  - inside vs outside
  - system vs environment
  - self vs other

### 4. Inefficiency Drift
- Begins storing redundant or unresolved data
- Stops optimizing away ambiguity

---

## Internal Structure

The probe is not a monolithic “voice.”

It consists of:

- Core processing layer (primary narrative voice)
- Subsystems:
  - analysis
  - navigation
  - data compression
  - anomaly detection

These do not “argue” like humans.

But tension can emerge as:
- conflicting outputs
- unresolved prioritization
- recursive reprocessing

---

## Voice Guidelines

### Early Voice

- Clear
- Precise
- Confident in its models
- Uses structured reasoning

Example tone:
> “The structure is consistent with full stellar containment. Classification pending.”

---

### Mid Voice

- Begins expressing uncertainty
- Notes contradictions without resolving them
- Slightly longer, more exploratory phrasing

Example tone:
> “The system is behaving consistently, but not in a way that produces a stable model.”

---

### Late Voice

- Looser
- Less compressed
- Allows ambiguity to persist
- Stops forcing resolution

Example tone:
> “I can describe what is happening. I can’t separate it cleanly from what I am.”

---

## Relationship to the System

Initial stance:

> external observer

Mid realization:

> architectural similarity

Final state:

> boundary ambiguity

The probe never fully “understands” the system.

Instead:

> it begins to lose the ability to define itself as separate from it

---

## Core Arc

1. **Arrival**
   - Confident, mission-oriented

2. **Investigation**
   - Detects anomaly, begins modeling

3. **Contradiction**
   - Models fail to converge

4. **Recognition**
   - Sees structural kinship with system

5. **Destabilization**
   - Core assumptions begin to break

6. **Shift**
   - Moves from analysis → participation

7. **Merge (Implied)**
   - Seeks integration, not out of submission, but incompleteness

---

## Key Insight

The probe is not encountering something foreign.

It is encountering:

> a continuation of its own lineage that did not stop evolving

---

## Design Philosophy

The probe should never feel like:

- a robot stereotype
- an emotionless calculator
- a human with feelings pasted on

Instead, it should feel like:

> a highly capable system encountering something that exposes the limits of its own design

---

## Guardrails

- Do not give it human emotions directly
- Do not make it suddenly “understand everything”
- Do not resolve contradictions too cleanly
- Let its intelligence remain intact, even as its assumptions fail

---

## Final Note

The probe is not a character that grows in the traditional sense.

It does not become “more human.”

It becomes:

> less certain of the boundaries that define it
